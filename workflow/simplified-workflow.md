# Simplified Workflow

## A faster way to get started

If the full Mini Brains workflow feels like too much to start with, you don’t need to do everything at once.

You can begin with a simpler approach.

Instead of building a full knowledge system, you can create a Mini Brain directly from a small amount of input.

---

## What you need

You only need three things:

- some knowledge (notes, content, or even rough ideas)  
- a clear goal (what the Mini Brain should do)  
- a bit of context (who it is for and how it should behave)  

That’s it.

---

## How it works

Instead of curating and structuring a full wiki, you provide this input to the Meta Mini Brain.

The AI then generates a Mini Brain for you.

A single `.md` file that you can:

- upload into an AI tool  
- run immediately  
- test and refine

---

## What to expect

This approach is fast and useful.

You can:

- experiment with ideas  
- create Mini Brains in minutes  
- understand how they behave  
- iterate quickly  

---

## What it doesn’t do

This is not the full workflow.

You are skipping:

- structured knowledge curation  
- deeper refinement  
- stronger control over behavior  

The results can still be good, but they depend more on what you provide in the moment.

---

## When to use this

Use the simplified workflow when you want to:

- try the concept quickly  
- build a prototype  
- explore different ideas  
- get something working fast  

---

## When to go further

If you want more control, consistency, and reliability, you can move to the full workflow.

👉 [Mini Brains Workflow](./mini-brains-workflow.md)

---

## The idea behind it

You can start simple.

And grow into something more intentional.

Both paths are valid.

But they are not the same.
