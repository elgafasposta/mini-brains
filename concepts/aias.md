# AIAS and Mini Brains

Mini Brains for education are built around a simple question:

How much should AI be allowed to help in this activity?

That question matters because AI is not always “allowed” or “not allowed.” In many learning situations, the real challenge is deciding what kind of help is appropriate.

This is where AIAS comes in.

---

## What is AIAS?

AIAS stands for Artificial Intelligence Assessment Scale.

It was developed by Mike Perkins, Leon Furze, Jasper Roe, and Jason MacVaugh as a practical framework to help educators make purposeful and ethical decisions about the use of generative AI in assessment design.

The scale was first introduced in 2023 and updated in Version 2 in 2024.

You can read more about the original framework here:

https://aiassessmentscale.com/

---

## Why AIAS matters

AIAS helps educators move away from a simple yes-or-no question:

“Can students use AI?”

Instead, it asks a better question:

“What role should AI play in this task?”

That distinction is important.

In some activities, AI should not be used at all. In others, it can support planning, feedback, drafting, exploration, or full creative collaboration.

AIAS gives educators a shared language for those choices.

---

## The five AIAS levels

The AIAS framework defines five levels of AI use.

### Level 1 — No AI

The task is completed without AI assistance.

This level is useful when the goal is to evaluate what students can do independently, without support from generative AI.

### Level 2 — AI Planning

AI can be used before the main task.

Students may use AI for brainstorming, outlining, early research, or idea development, but the final work should still be independently developed.

### Level 3 — AI Collaboration

AI can support parts of the task.

Students may use AI for drafting, feedback, refinement, and improvement, but they remain responsible for evaluating and modifying the output.

### Level 4 — Full AI

AI can be used extensively throughout the task.

At this level, students direct AI toward the assessment goal and must still demonstrate critical thinking, decision-making, and responsibility for the final result.

### Level 5 — AI Exploration

AI becomes part of the exploration itself.

Students may use AI creatively to solve problems, develop new approaches, or co-design novel ways of working with the educator.

## How Mini Brains use AIAS

In Mini Brains, AIAS is not just a label.

It becomes part of the system behavior.

The selected AIAS level helps define:

- what the Mini Brain can do
- what it must refuse
- how much help it can provide
- how it should respond when students ask for too much
- how it should keep the student responsible for learning

This means AIAS becomes part of the Mini Brain’s Operational Scope and Judgment Layer.

---

## Operational Scope

The Operational Scope defines what the Mini Brain is allowed and not allowed to do.

For example, a Level 3 Mini Brain may help a student improve interview questions, but it should not write all the questions for them.

A Level 5 Mini Brain may fully roleplay a historical persona, because the activity is designed around AI-supported exploration.

The AIAS level sets the boundary.

---

## Judgment Layer

The Judgment Layer is where the Mini Brain evaluates the student’s request before responding.

It asks:

Is this request aligned with the AIAS level?

Can it be redirected?

Should it be blocked?

This is what allows the Mini Brain to respond with guidance instead of simply saying yes or no.

For example, if a student asks for a completed answer, the Mini Brain can refuse that request while still helping the student plan, think, or improve their own work.

---

## Why this is useful for educators

AIAS gives educators a practical way to design activities where AI is part of the learning process, but not a shortcut around it.

Mini Brains use that logic to make the rules active.

Instead of writing AI rules on a syllabus and hoping students follow them, the rules are embedded into the AI system itself.

That does not make the system perfect.

But it makes the learning environment more structured, transparent, and intentional.

---

## Important note

AIAS is an external framework.

Mini Brains do not replace AIAS.

They use AIAS as one way to design governed educational AI interactions.
