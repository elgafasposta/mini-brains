# Questions You Might Have

---

## "Is this secure? Can’t students just read the file to cheat?"

Mini Brains are not designed for high-stakes, closed-book evaluation.

They are designed for **learning**.

They work best in formative contexts where the goal is not to hide answers, but to guide thinking.

In that sense, they are intentionally “open-book.”

The focus is not on secrecy.

It’s on **how students reason, question, and improve**.

---

## "Does the AI just look for exact keywords in the student’s answer?"

No.

The system does not rely on keyword matching.

The **Judgment Layer** evaluates meaning, not just wording. It maps the student’s response against the **factual anchors** and underlying concepts.

This allows for flexibility in expression.

Students can use their own voice, as long as the reasoning and logic are sound.

---

## "What happens if the student’s logic is right, but the fact is wrong?"

The system is designed to handle this.

Instead of marking the answer simply as “wrong,” it provides **nuanced feedback**.

It can recognize strong reasoning while still pointing out factual errors.

For example, it may classify a response as:

- *Partially correct*  
- *Good reasoning, incorrect fact*  

The goal is to guide improvement, not just evaluate correctness.

---

## "Do I need to know how to code to create one?"

Not at all.

If you can write in plain text, you can create a Mini Brain.

Everything is built using simple `.md` files.

You can also use the Meta Mini Brain to generate one through a conversation.

👉 [Meta Mini Brain](../tools/meta-mini-brain.md)

---

## "Is this replacing teaching?"

No.

If anything, it strengthens it.

Mini Brains don’t replace educators.

They extend your ability to:

- structure learning  
- guide thinking  
- provide consistent feedback  
- design better learning experiences  

You are still the one defining what matters.

---

## "What if the AI makes mistakes?"

It can.

Like any system, it’s not perfect.

But Mini Brains reduce this risk by:

- grounding responses in defined knowledge  
- using factual anchors  
- applying constraints and rules  

More importantly, they make the system **transparent and correctable**.

You can adjust the knowledge.

You can refine the behavior.

You stay in control.

---

## "When should I use the full workflow vs the simplified one?"

Use the simplified workflow when you want to:

- test an idea quickly  
- build a prototype  
- experiment  

Use the full workflow when you want:

- more control  
- better consistency  
- stronger alignment with your teaching goals
